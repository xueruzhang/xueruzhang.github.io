I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at The Ohio State University. I am also affiliated with the Translational Data Analytics Institute and Center on Responsible AI and Governance.

At OSU, I lead the Ethical and Trustworthy Human-Intelligent Computing (ETHIC) Lab, where we advance the theoretical and algorithmic foundations of trustworthy machine learning in high-stakes, dynamic, multi-agent, and human-interactive environments. This includes (1) understanding the societal and economic impacts of ML systems; (2) developing principled methods that enhance algorithmic fairness, privacy, robustness, safety, interpretability, and alignment with human values; and (3) designing steering and incentive mechanisms that guide multi-agent and human-ML interactions toward socially beneficial outcomes. These efforts have broad interdisciplinary impact across healthcare, earth sciences, finance, transportation, and social domains.

For more details, please see my group and publications. Our research is generously supported by the National Science Foundation, Cisco, Center for Clinical and Translational Science, and Nationwide Children's Hospital, and OSU Translational Data Analytics Institute.

Prior to joining OSU, I completed my PhD under the supervision of Mingyan Liu at the University of Michigan.
